# Check Please

**Register:** brand

## Product purpose

A player just lost on chess.com, wants to know what went wrong, and hits the paywall. This site
takes them from arriving to a running engine analysis on Lichess in under ten seconds, on a phone,
on a slow connection, having never seen it before.

Everything else is secondary to that path. A feature that lengthens it gets cut.

## Users

Club-level and casual chess.com players. They are annoyed, they are on a phone, and they are one
tab away from giving up. They are not browsing. They arrived with a job.

## Voice

Blunt, mechanical, quick. Plain verbs, sentence case, active voice. Buttons say what happens.
No filler, no exclamation marks, no growth-marketing verbs.

Physical reference: the pairing sheet taped to a wall at a weekend swiss, and the flag on a
mechanical chess clock a second before it falls.

## Strategic principles

- The primary action is a plain link. No spinner can sit between a click and the analysis.
- Nothing is stored on a server, because there is no server. Say so plainly.
- Lichess does the actual work and is a nonprofit. Credit them warmly and link their donations.
- Public data only, through chess.com's public API.

## Anti-references

Explicitly off-limits, from the brief:

- Cream background, high-contrast serif, terracotta accent.
- Near-black with a single acid accent. (Including amber. A one-accent-on-ink palette is this
  pattern with the hue swapped.)
- Broadsheet layout, hairline rules, dense columns.
- Checkerboards, Staunton knight silhouettes, 8x8 grids. The first thing anyone reaches for.

The richer vein: notation, clocks, score sheets, crosstables, tournament bulletins, opening trees,
evaluation bars.
